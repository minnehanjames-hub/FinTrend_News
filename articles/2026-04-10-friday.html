Friday Recap: CPI Relief, Oil at $98,
and a Ceasefire Nobody Trusts

Markets got what they needed this morning — and still couldn't decide what to do with it. Here's what actually changed today and what to watch going into next week.

Markets got what they needed this morning — and still couldn't decide what to do with it.

The April CPI print came in at 3.8% headline and 2.8% core, both in line with expectations. No nasty surprise. No acceleration. In a normal year that would barely move the tape. In April 2026, with oil near $100 and a five-week Middle East conflict still simmering, it was enough to trigger a morning rally in the S&P and Nasdaq. The market's message was simple: the greatest fear right now isn't inflation — it's uncertainty. Remove the uncertainty, even slightly, and money comes back in.

"The market's greatest fear right now isn't inflation — it's uncertainty. Remove the uncertainty, even slightly, and money comes back in."

But the relief didn't hold cleanly. By afternoon stocks were wavering as reports emerged that Trump is actively preparing military options if this weekend's Pakistan peace talks with Iran fall apart. The ceasefire that sent markets up 2.5% on Wednesday is fragile. Oil, which briefly dipped below $95, climbed back toward $98 as traders remembered that Iran is still controlling access to the Strait of Hormuz, ceasefire or not. The S&P settled around 6,813 — up fractionally on the week, but well off the session highs.

Three Things That Actually Matter Next Week

Key Catalysts · Week of April 13
  • Pakistan Peace Talks (this weekend) — First direct US-Iran dialogue since the ceasefire. A breakdown doesn't just mean oil spikes — the entire relief rally of the past week gets reversed in a single session. A breakthrough means oil cracks below $90 and rate-cut bets surge.
  • Bank Earnings — JPM, WFC, BLK (Friday April 17) — Dimon's commentary on credit quality and consumer health is the most important data point of the quarter. If reserves are building, the recession trade is back on. If the quarter is clean, the S&P has room to run toward 7,000.
  • Fed Speakers Throughout the Week — Core PCE is already running hot at 2.8%. With oil near $100 and CPI at 3.8%, the May cut that markets were pricing in six weeks ago has been pushed back. Every 10bps shift in the 2-year moves tech valuations by roughly 3%.

The Honest Positioning Call for the Weekend

The S&P is at 6,813. We're up roughly 7% from the March lows. That's a significant bounce off levels that were pricing in real recession risk. The CPI data today removes one tail risk. But it doesn't remove oil at $98, it doesn't remove geopolitical binary risk this weekend, and it doesn't change the fact that corporate margins are getting squeezed from both sides — input costs up, consumer demand softening.

The right posture going into next week is not to chase this rally. Hold your defensive positions — TLT for duration, GLD for the flight-to-safety trade, LMT for the geopolitical hedge. Keep dry powder for a potential dip on Pakistan talks breakdown. And watch JPMorgan's earnings call closely. That's your real tell on where this economy actually is versus where the market thinks it is.
