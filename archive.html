Energy ↑

via Brent spot, Bloomberg · Mar 2
Inflation ↑
February PPI came in hotter than expected. Core services remain sticky. The read-through to PCE — the Fed's preferred gauge — complicated the rate-cut narrative heading into March.
via BLS PPI release · Feb 27
Tech ↓
Nvidia extended its post-earnings slide despite beating estimates. Guidance was underwhelming on margin expansion. The AI monetization question isn't resolved — the stock is now base-building territory not momentum territory.
via Nvidia earnings call · Feb 26
Fed Watch
Markets are now pricing just one cut in 2026. The combination of hot PPI, sticky PCE, and a geopolitical oil shock makes the Fed's path narrower. Watch NFP Friday — a strong number removes any near-term cut optionality.
via CME FedWatch · Mar 2
Geopolitical
Ukraine-Russia ceasefire talks remain in framework stage — nothing signed. Iran strike risk premium now embedded in oil and defense. Middle East escalation timeline is the wildcard that can override any technical setup.
via Reuters, FT · Mar 1–2
Gold ↑
Gold continued its structural bid — now up over 12% YTD. Central bank buying (China, India) remains the structural floor. Geopolitical bid adds a second layer. $2,900 was breached briefly — $3,000 is the psychological next test.
via COMEX, WGC data · Mar 2
